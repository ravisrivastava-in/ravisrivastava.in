BCA-Department

The University Grant Commission (UGC), New Delhi,approved an introduction of Computer Application to the premier college in the very first phase in 1994 as three year degree (Honors) course. Several batches of Computer Applications students have already passed out and are well placed with prominent National and Multinational companies in India and abroad or are pursuing higher education in prominent institutes.

Aim & Objective

The aims and objectives of the department/institution are given below :

  • To produce computer professionals with thorough knowledge, professional confidence and analytical skills to take up the challenges of the dynamic computer and IT industry.
  • To develop leadership skills and spirit of excellence in our students.
  • To prepare students to face the dynamic IT industry and its challenges.
  • To prepare students as IT Industry ready.
  • To prepare students as a good programmers.

    • Markham College Of Commerce, Hazaribag 

    Markham College of Commerce caters to the educational needs of students in Hazaribag district in India. It initiated commerce teaching up to degree level in its North Chotanagpur headquarters.
        
                            It was founded on February 10, 1974 by B. D. Jaiswal.
                            
                            It was named after educationist Arthur Francis Markham who came to India with his goal to make higher education to the students of the area.

                            Markham was ordained with the Order of British Empire. He later became the Vice Chancellor of the Ranchi University. Even after his retirement, he remained engaged with the students.
                            In 1974, the first session of Intermediate Arts and Commerce was started. Three years later, Ranchi awarded affiliation for the subjects Computer Applications, Hindi,English,Sanskrit,
                            History, Economics,Political Science, Philosophy and Commerce up to degree level.The college was established in the late 1970s. It was founded by Shiv Dayal Singh and named after 
                            ex-principal Sir Markham Sahab. The college initially had 5 students and has now[when?] grown to a strength of 10,000 students soon after.
                            
                            At the time of foundation, Dr. Maheshwar Tiwari, who as a founder Principal and in charge of the

                            Prof. Sabita Banerjee (Deptt.Of Philosophy), Prof. Aparna Banerjee (Deptt. of Sanskrit), Prof. Nitay Nand Choudhary (Deptt. of Commerce).
